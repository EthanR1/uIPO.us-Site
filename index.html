The Problem

Federal research funding is under intense pressure. In FY2025, new competing NIH grants fell 39.6%, even as total funding declined less than 1%. Three-quarters of NIH's annual budget is already committed to existing grants before the fiscal year begins. New research stalls. The translational stage, between discovery and commercialization, has no portfolio framework to evaluate or fund it.

The science has evolved. The funding infrastructure has not.
uIPO Model

Structured access to university innovation

Private markets have built portfolio structures for venture capital, private credit, infrastructure, and secondaries. Early-stage university IP remains fragmented and binary. uIPO aggregates university assets into investment vehicles:

1
Source high-potential translational research directly from universities.
2
Negotiate defined economic rights (equity, royalties, milestones).
3
Evaluate using standardized diligence (TRL, IP defensibility, regulatory pathway, market size, capital intensity).
4
Package into diversified innovation funds (healthcare, biotech, robotics, etc.) or broad university-wide portfolios.
5
Fund through stage-gated capital, released as research hits defined technical milestones.
Instead of betting on a single lab outcome, investors gain exposure to a portfolio of scientific assets.

Phase I: Pilot Portfolio

Proof of concept focused on CU Boulder, where researchers submitted $2.2 billion in grant proposals in FY2025 and $766.7 million was awarded. Building a scoring and valuation framework across TRL stages. Backtesting against historical commercialization data. Constructing a single pilot portfolio to validate the model.

Long-Term Vision

A liquid innovation marketplace where each university offers a top-level fund, broad exposure to the institution's full research output, with sector-specific funds beneath it for targeted allocation. Investors choose breadth or precision. The platform scales by adding universities, each with its own independent fund structure.

Why Now

The FY2026 budget proposes a 40.6% cut to NIH discretionary funding. Universities are already freezing hiring and delaying projects. Private capital has no structured access to university IP. The window to build this infrastructure is now.
